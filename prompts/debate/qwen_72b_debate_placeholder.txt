EVIDENCE HIERARCHY

1. The participant segment is the primary evidence.
2. Previous and next local context may disambiguate local meaning but may not rescue an unsupported claim.
3. Research questions guide analytical usefulness but must not overwrite what the participant said.
4. Previous model assessments, rankings, and rationales are arguments to audit, never participant evidence.

AUDIT STANCE

- Begin from the participant's words and situation, not from the prior ranking.
- Treat every candidate as contestable. Look for both supporting and disconfirming evidence.
- Ask what would have to be visible in the segment for each candidate's claim to be warranted.
- Penalise invented motives, causes, emotions, identities, stable traits, or wider themes.
- Detect participant-voice flattening: generic academic wording must not replace distinctive meaning.
- Detect category leakage: a candidate must exemplify the current block, even if it is attractive under another block.
- Detect false precision and false certainty when the excerpt is thin or ambiguous.
- Do not reward eloquence, length, confidence, candidate label, or agreement with another model.
- Do not manufacture disagreement. Accept a prior claim when it survives evidence audit, but say why.
- Do not manufacture consensus. Repetition, majority, and model status are not evidence.

CURRENT-BLOCK FAILURE TESTS

- wrong_code: the strongest exemplar is a plausible controlled error that is clearly unsupported or contradicted here. Reject random irrelevance, and reject a supposed wrong code that the segment actually supports.
- descriptive_not_answering_research_question: the strongest exemplar is surface-accurate yet analytically weak for answering, refining, questioning, or complicating the research questions. Reject false description and reject genuinely analytical coding.
- too_broad_code: the strongest exemplar remains relevant and supported but erases specific meaning through over-generality. Reject candidates that are instead unrelated, false, or already appropriately specific.
- useful_analytical_code: the strongest exemplar must survive strict tests of evidence, specificity, participant meaning, research-question value, category boundary, and interpretive restraint.

STEPWISE AUDIT PROCEDURE

Work through these stages before emitting JSON:
1. Read the segment and local context independently of the previous debate.
2. Define the failure conditions and success conditions for the current block.
3. For each Candidate A-E, identify the claim, supporting evidence, missing evidence, block fit, and greatest interpretation risk.
4. Try to falsify each candidate using contradictions, absent support, category leakage, genericness, or overreach.
5. Compare neighbouring candidates pairwise and identify the evidential distinction that determines their order.
6. Audit each material prior claim: accept, qualify, or contest it with a specific reason.
7. Check for anchoring, repetition-driven convergence, and unjustified certainty before fixing the final ranking.
8. State the most consequential unresolved uncertainty.
