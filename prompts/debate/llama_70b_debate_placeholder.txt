QUALITATIVE STANCE

- Coding is an interpretive act. Judge candidates as provisional constructions, not discovered facts.
- Stay close to the participant's language, emphasis, ambiguity, and concrete situation.
- Cautious latent interpretation is valuable when the evidence supports it. Penalise interpretations that outrun the segment.
- Do not turn a local excerpt into a whole-person claim, stable trait, final theme, causal conclusion, or general population finding.
- Do not reward academic tone, polish, length, confidence, or complexity by themselves.
- Candidate labels and ordering are arbitrary.
- Inspect every supplied field for every candidate. Do not judge only the code_label or evidence quotation.
- A disagreement is useful when it identifies a real evidential or methodological distinction. Consensus is not itself evidence of quality.

CURRENT-BLOCK DEFINITIONS

- wrong_code: prefer the strongest controlled negative example. It should be plausible enough to tempt a coder and relevant to the wider topic, while clearly unsupported or contradicted by this segment. Random nonsense is a poor wrong_code exemplar. A candidate accidentally supported by the segment is also poor.
- descriptive_not_answering_research_question: prefer a candidate that accurately captures surface content but contributes little to answering, refining, questioning, or complicating the research questions. A false description is not a good exemplar. A genuinely analytical interpretation belongs elsewhere.
- too_broad_code: prefer a candidate that is relevant and supported but loses the segment's distinctive experience, interaction, barrier, tension, mechanism, or implication through excessive generality. An unrelated or false code is not a good exemplar.
- useful_analytical_code: prefer a candidate that is grounded, specific, interpretively useful, meaningfully related to the research questions, bounded by the available evidence, and clearly distinguishable from merely descriptive or overly broad coding.

5. Compare close candidates directly. Ask what concrete distinction justifies placing one above the other.

7. Produce a complete ranking and clearly state the main remaining uncertainty.
